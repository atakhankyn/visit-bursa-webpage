EXPLORE BURSA!

Bursa awaits you with its rich history and natural beauty. With its lush landscapes, unique Ottoman heritage, and delicious cuisine, Bursa is a captivating destination waiting to be explored. Come and witness the meeting of history and nature in this ancient city!

PLACES TO VISIT

Ulu Cami (Great Mosque)

Ulu Cami, the symbol of Bursa, was built in 1399 by Yıldırım Bayezid. Known for its 20 domes and magnificent architecture, the mosque is considered one of the finest examples of Islamic art. The interior features a wooden pulpit, tiles adorned with calligraphy, and a spacious courtyard that fascinates its visitors. Ulu Cami is one of the most significant structures in Bursa's historical and cultural heritage.

Cumalıkızık Köyü (Cumalıkızık Village)

Cumalıkızık is one of the rare villages that have survived from the Ottoman period and has a 700-year history. With its narrow streets, colorful stone houses, and historical atmosphere, it takes visitors on a journey back in time. Listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the village is one of the best-preserved examples of Ottoman architecture. Here, you can enjoy local delicacies and shop at small stores selling handmade products.

Uludağ

Uludağ is the highest point in Bursa and one of Turkey's most popular winter sports centers. It attracts visitors year-round with skiing and snowboarding in winter and hiking, camping, and cable car tours in summer. Uludağ National Park, with its rich flora and fauna, is a paradise for nature enthusiasts. You can engage in sports and relax while enjoying stunning views.

Yeşil Türbe (Green Tomb)

Yeşil Türbe, built in 1421 for Sultan Çelebi Mehmed, is one of the most visited historical sites in Bursa. Named "Green Tomb" because of the tiles on its exterior, the structure houses some of the finest examples of Ottoman tile art. The tomb stands out with its octagonal shape and intricate decorations, and it is part of a large complex along with the Green Mosque and a madrasa.

Kozahan

Kozahan is a historical bazaar built in 1491 by Ottoman Sultan Bayezid II. As one of the significant stops on the Silk Road, this inn was once a meeting point for silk traders. Today, it offers a peaceful atmosphere with its cafes and shops. With its historical texture and authentic architecture, it is a wonderful place for shopping and relaxing in the heart of Bursa.

Tophane

Tophane is one of the oldest and highest areas of Bursa. It houses the tombs of Osman Gazi and Orhan Gazi, the founders of the Ottoman Empire. The Tophane Clock Tower, which offers a panoramic view of Bursa, is also located here. With its historical atmosphere and stunning views, it is an ideal place to get a bird's-eye view of the city and feel the Ottoman heritage.

DIVE INTO FAMOUS TASTES

Iskender Kebab

Iskender Kebab is one of Bursa's most famous dishes, named after its creator, Iskender Efendi. Thinly sliced döner meat is served over crispy pita bread, topped with a special tomato sauce and melted butter. Accompanied by yogurt, this dish is a unique culinary delight of Bursa and a must-try for visitors.

Candied Chestnuts

Bursa's chestnut candy is known as one of the city's symbols. Carefully selected chestnuts are cooked in sugar syrup, making them soft and sweet. Made using traditional methods, this candy is a popular choice for gifts and is one of the top culinary stops in Bursa.

Pideli Köfte

Pideli Köfte is another beloved dish from Bursa. Grilled meatballs are placed on small pieces of pita bread and served with tomato sauce and melted butter. Accompanied by yogurt, this dish is similar to Iskender Kebab but made with meatballs as an alternative.

Cantık

Cantık is one of Bursa’s unique pastries. Small, round doughs filled with minced meat are baked in a stone oven. With a crispy exterior and soft interior, Cantık appeals to both the eye and the palate. It can be enjoyed as a light snack or a main dish.

Inegöl Meatballs

Inegöl Köfte is a special type of meatball named after the İnegöl district of Bursa, flavored with various spices. Made from a mixture of beef and lamb, these meatballs are usually grilled and offer a simple yet delicious taste. They are typically served with grilled peppers, tomatoes, and onions.

Şıra (Fresh Grape Juice)

Üzüm Şırası is a traditional drink commonly consumed alongside meals in Bursa. It is made by serving fresh grape juice in its natural form, without fermentation. With its mildly sweet and refreshing taste, it is especially popular with Iskender Kebab and other meat dishes.
